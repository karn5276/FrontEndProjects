High School Level Exams
SAT Subject Tests

Purpose: Assess your knowledge in particular subjects for college admissions.
Subjects: Available in Math, Sciences (Biology, Chemistry, Physics), History, Literature, Languages, and more.
Format: Mostly multiple-choice questions, except Language Tests with Listening.
Scores: Range from 200 to 800 per test.
Duration: Most tests are one hour long, except for Language Tests.
Test Dates: Usually coincide with regular SAT dates, but subject availability varies.
Usage: Some colleges require/recommend them for admission or placement.
Score Choice: You can select which scores to send to colleges.
Preparation: Study subject material, use official practice tests, and understand the test structure.
Languages with Listening: Some language tests include listening sections.
Importance: Check specific college requirements before deciding which tests to take.

University Entrance Exams
SAT (Scholastic Assessment Test)

Purpose : Assess readiness for college and provide a common data point for comparing all applicants.
Sections: It consists of Evidence-Based Reading and Writing (including reading and writing/language), and Math.
Format: Multiple-choice questions and an optional essay (Essay scores are separate).
Scores: Composite score ranges from 400 to 1600 (combining Math and Evidence-Based Reading and Writing sections).
Duration: About 3 hours without the essay and 3 hours 50 minutes with the essay.
Use: Many colleges require or consider SAT scores during the admissions process.
Preparation: Studying, practice tests, and understanding test-taking strategies can improve scores.
Flexibility: You can choose which scores to send to colleges (Score Choice).
Test Dates: Offered multiple times a year.
Importance: Alongside other factors, SAT scores can influence college acceptance and scholarship opportunities.
Adaptive: Questions get harder or easier based on your answers within a section.
Essay: The optional essay measures reading, analysis, and writing skills.
